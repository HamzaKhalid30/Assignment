</br>**Overview**</br>
In my home environment, I focused on analyzing the security of my Wi-Fi network. I found that my network uses WPA3 encryption, which is currently one of the most secure wireless security protocols.

</br>**Practical**</br>
1.	Open Wi-Fi settings on your phone/laptop </br>
2.	Connect to your home Wi-Fi </br>
3.	Open network details</br>
4.	Control Panel → Network & Internet </br>
5.	Network and Sharing Center </br>
6.	Click your Wi-Fi </br>
7.	Click Wireless Properties → Security tab</br>

</br>**Findings and Details**</br>
•	My home Wi-Fi is configured using WPA3 (Wi-Fi Protected Access 3), which provides strong encryption and secure authentication.</br>
•	WPA3 protects data transmitted over the network, ensuring that even if someone intercepts the traffic, they cannot easily read or modify it.</br>
•	It introduces advanced security features such as improved encryption and protection against brute-force attacks </br>
•	Compared to older protocols like WPA2 or WEP, WPA3 significantly reduces the risk of attacks such as password cracking and man-in-the-middle attacks.</br>
•	This ensures that communication between devices (mobile, laptop, router) remains confidential and secure.</br>
•	Additionally, modern home security practices recommend combining WPA3 with network segmentation and strong passwords for better protection </br>

</br>**Reflection**</br>
This activity helped me understand the importance of using modern encryption standards like WPA3. Securing a home network is essential because it acts as the entry point for all connected devices. Strong encryption ensures that attackers cannot easily intercept or exploit network communications.
