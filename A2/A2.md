## Findings and Details

- I discovered that certain public surveillance cameras are accessible over the internet due to misconfiguration or lack of proper authentication.
- To explore this further, I used a tool called Shodan, which is a search engine for internet-connected devices. It allows users to find exposed systems such as cameras, routers, and servers.
- Using Shodan, I was able to identify devices that were publicly accessible, including camera interfaces without strong authentication.
- This highlights a major issue: many devices are deployed with default credentials or open ports, making them vulnerable to unauthorized access.
- Research shows that exposed cameras can leak sensitive information such as video feeds, personal data, and location details, leading to serious privacy risks.
- Insecure configurations like lack of encryption, weak passwords, or open access interfaces are common reasons for such exposure.

## Reflection

This activity made me realize that misconfigured public systems can become easy targets for attackers. Even security devices like cameras can turn into vulnerabilities if not properly secured. Proper configuration, authentication, and regular monitoring are essential to ensure safety in public environments.
