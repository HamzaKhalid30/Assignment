# A17: Discover Two Hallucination Cases in Generative AI

## Overview

AI hallucination occurs when a generative AI system produces incorrect or misleading information that appears to be accurate. This is a major concern in AI security and reliability.

## Practical

1. Interacted with AI tools (e.g., chatbots).
2. Asked complex or factual questions.
3. Verified responses using trusted sources.

## Findings and Details

### Case 1: Incorrect Technical Information
- Asked AI about a cybersecurity concept.
- AI provided partially incorrect explanation.
- Verified using official sources → found mismatch.

### Case 2: Fake Reference/Source
- Asked AI for references.
- AI generated non-existent articles or links.
- Confirmed that sources were not real.

## Reflection

This activity helped me understand the limitations of AI systems. It showed that AI cannot always be trusted blindly and must be verified. This is important in cybersecurity where incorrect information can lead to serious risks.
